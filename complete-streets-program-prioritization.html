Home  >  Projects & Clients  >  Projects  >   Complete Streets Program Prioritization

In 2014, the Jacksonville Transportation Authority (JTA) overhauled its transit system to provide more frequent, reliable service. This redesign is known as the Route Optimization Initiative (ROI). To complement ROI and address first and last mile issues, the JTA implemented a Complete Streets-based infrastructure initiative called Mobility Corridors. These Mobility Corridors represent high frequency transit routes designated under the ROI. The program is intended to maximize all functions of the street, putting people and “quality of place” first, with an understanding of the importance of the street to transit access and overall mobility. Access and mobility have important implications for safety, health, economic development, and equity.

 

The Jacksonville Transportation Authority (JTA) desires a strategic update of its Complete Streets effort. The JTA established a prioritization system to advance potential projects from the identified focus areas within each of the Mobility Corridors. The ranking criteria reflected a comprehensive list of transit characteristics, safety factors, economic development potential, health and equity, planning consistency, funding resources, as well as social and cultural amenities as a basis for project sequencing.

C2G was subcontracted by Michael Baker to prepare a best in industry review of equity metrics used by transportation agencies and to make recommendations for the JTA’s Complete Streets Mobility Corridors program. C2G summarized the results in a white paper entitled, Prioritizing Equity in the JTA’s Complete Streets Program.

 

C2G staff were actively engaged in:

  • Best practice review of equity metrics
  • Assessment of equity metrics
  • Recommendations and report preparation
